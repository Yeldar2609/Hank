# Hank

Hank is my custom useless box project that I designed in Fusion and then modified to fit the parts I actually had access to. I originally found a useless box design online, but after measuring my servo and switches, I realized a lot of the original dimensions would not work, so I ended up redesigning multiple parts instead of printing it as-is.

I am using:
- 1 servo motor
- 1 two-position toggle switch
- 1 limit switch
- 2 AAA batteries

Most of my time so far has gone into measuring components, redesigning parts, and testing fitment. I made multiple switch versions (V1, V2, and V3) because my first designs either didn’t line up correctly or didn’t leave enough room for movement. I also had to redesign the motor housing and resize the main box to make sure the arm could move without hitting the walls.

One of the biggest things I learned was optimizing 3D printing. My first box print was estimated at 24 hours using a 0.15mm nozzle. After testing settings and doing research, I switched to a 0.2mm nozzle and changed supports to build plate only, which brought the print down to about 9 hours while still looking good.

Right now the printed parts are done, and I am moving into wiring, soldering, and testing the final mechanism.
